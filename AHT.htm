    Average Handle Time



    Average Handle Time (AHT) - is the average total duration of a customer interaction, from its start to its conclusion. It includes talk time, when an agent speaks with the customer; hold time, when the customer is on hold; and after-call work (ACW), which involves tasks like updating records or making notes after the customer interaction ends. AHT is a key performance indicator (KPI) used to measure an agent's efficiency and the call center's overall operational performance. A lower AHT generally means agents can handle more calls, which indicates higher efficiency. Efficient AHT can help manage operational costs by optimizing staffing levels.

    Components of AHT:

    • Talk Time : The time an agent spends actively conversing with a customer.
    • Hold Time : The time a customer waits on hold during the interaction.
    • After-Call Work (ACW) & Extended Wrap (EXT) : Tasks performed by the agent after the customer interaction concludes to resolve the call.


    How AHT is Calculated:

      AHT  =   Total Talk Time + Total Hold Time + Total After-Call Work
